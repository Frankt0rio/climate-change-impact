Displacement

In 2023, British Columbia experienced a record number of wildfire evacuations, with tens of thousands of people forced to leave their homes. While many were able to return after the immediate danger passed, some Indigenous communities are now facing the possibility of permanent relocation. These climate emergencies are not isolated incidents but are tied to a longer history of colonialism. In the past, many First Nations were removed from their traditional territories and placed on reserves that were often located in areas prone to flooding and other environmental risks. Today, those same reserves are increasingly threatened by climate change, putting the safety and future of these communities at even greater risk.

Critical Thinking

Being stripped of one's identity is the one worst thing you can do as a human being, you are also removing their sovereignty and control. When discussing climate change, it should be under indigenous leadership, as they are the ones that truly understand the needs of humanity to a deeper level.
