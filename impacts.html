Wildfires.

Wildfires in BC

British Columbia has been facing terrible and destructive wildfire seasons, with 2023 being the worst in BC's recorded history. The 2023 wildfire season burnt a total of 2.84 million hectares of forest and land. This caused tens of thousands of people to evacuate and hundreds of homes damaged/destroyed. Indigenous communities are losing sacred forests to these fires as well as killing important wildlife.

Floods & Extreme Weather

The current weather we are experiencing is breaking and exceeding past records. For example, the flood that occurred in November 2021 that submerged farmland and areas all across Fraser Valley that ended up causing the death of six-hundred-thousand farm animals and washed out highways, making some communities unreachable. First Nations like the Okanagan had to relocate after their homes were flooded after the Nicola River overflowed. Fisheries & Food

The rising temperatures can cause changes in flow which leads to reducing the survival of certain species in the water. So is the case of salmon, which is predicted to fall by 50% in Indigenous Peopleâ€™s fisheries. This is because salmon need cold water streams, not warm water. Warm water also literally makes salmon less likely to even spawn. Outside of the water, the seasons that are becoming increasingly unpredictable are causing berries to ripen at odd times and/or fail to after experiencing heat stress. Even animals like moose are moving as the forage declines. All of these changes together represent a huge problem, as it is threatening food security as well as erasing cultural activities such as hunting or fishing.

Displacement.

Displacement & Cultural Loss

Many communities lie close to rivers or coasts which exposes them to the risks that climate change is bringing. This risk can and will cause displacement in the future which consequently, can cause loss of culture. When communities relocate, they leave behind sacred ground which could carry stories or spiritual meaning. Coastline communities are not the only ones affected as communities that live in forests can experience catastrophic fires and destroy whole villages (Like Lytton)
