Nov 2019: UN Declaration Act Passed

British Columbia enacts the Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples Act, committing to align all provincial laws with the United Nations Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples.

2019: First Nations Declare a Climate Emergency

The Union of BC Indian Chiefs and the BC Assembly of First Nations recognize a climate emergency. They call for a joint BC First Nations Climate Strategy and Action Plan to address the urgent environmental problems.

June 2021: Record Heatwave and Lytton Fire

An unprecedented heat dome pushes temperatures in Lytton, on Nlaka'pamux land, to a staggering 49.6°C. The following day, a wildfire fueled by the heatwave destroys the village.

Nov 2021: Atmospheric River Floods

A series of Pacific storms unleashes catastrophic flooding across southwestern BC, drowning thousands of farm animals and destroying countless homes, roads, and highways.

Early 2022: BC First Nations Climate Strategy Published

First Nations Leadership Council releases a comprehensive BC First Nations Climate Strategy and Action Plan. Endorsed unanimously by Chief and Council assemblies.

April 2022: Heiltsuk Clean Energy Initiative

The Heiltsuk Tribal Council completes a community energy plan backed by $5 million in funding. Approximately 250 Bella Bella homes will receive energy-efficient heat pumps.

May 2023: Great Bear Rainforest Protections Finalized

This landmark agreement secures the conservation of 6.4 million hectares of forest and leaves the Indigenous people to lead it.

June 2023: BC First Nations Climate Action Fund

The BC government announces a $10 million fund to support First Nations in their climate action efforts. The fund will be used for projects that reduce greenhouse gas emissions and build resilience to climate change.
